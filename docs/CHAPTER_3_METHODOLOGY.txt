3.1 Research Methodology

This study employed the Agile methodology, specifically the Scrum framework, as the development approach for the Parish Operational Records Management System with ML Kit OCR. Agile was chosen because it allows for iterative development, continuous feedback, and flexibility in accommodating changes throughout the development process. This approach is particularly suitable for software development projects where requirements may evolve as stakeholders gain better understanding of the system capabilities.

The development process was divided into several sprints, each lasting approximately two to three weeks. Each sprint focused on delivering specific functional components of the system, starting with core authentication and user management features, followed by record management capabilities, OCR integration, and finally administrative functions. This incremental approach allowed for continuous testing and refinement of features, ensuring that each component was fully functional before proceeding to the next phase.

The phases of the methodology included: (1) Planning and Requirements Gathering, where initial system requirements were collected through interviews and observation; (2) Design and Architecture, where the system structure, database schema, and user interface mockups were created; (3) Development, where actual coding and feature implementation took place in iterative sprints; (4) Testing, where unit tests, integration tests, and user acceptance testing were conducted; and (5) Deployment and Evaluation, where the system was deployed and evaluated by end-users. Each phase contributed to the overall quality and functionality of the final system, ensuring that it met the needs of the parish staff and administrators.

3.2 Data Gathering Procedure

The data gathering process was conducted to understand the current record management practices at the parish and to identify the specific requirements for the proposed system. The methods used included interviews, direct observation, and review of existing documents and records.

3.2.1 Research Instrument

The primary research instruments used in this study were:

1. Interview Guide: A semi-structured interview guide was developed to conduct in-depth interviews with the parish priest, parish staff, and administrative personnel. The interview guide contained open-ended questions about current record-keeping practices, challenges faced in managing sacramental records, frequency of record requests, and desired features for a digital system. The guide also explored specific pain points such as time spent searching for records, common errors in manual entries, and security concerns with paper-based storage.

2. Observation Checklist: An observation checklist was used during site visits to document the current workflow of record management. This included observing how staff receive, process, file, and retrieve baptismal, marriage, and confirmation records. The checklist captured information about physical storage conditions, record organization methods, and the typical steps involved when parishioners request certificates.

3. Document Review Form: A structured form was used to review sample sacramental records, noting the types of information recorded, variations in record formats, physical condition of documents, and volume of records maintained over different time periods.

These instruments collected qualitative data about user needs, operational workflows, and specific requirements for OCR text extraction from existing sacramental documents.

3.2.2 Sampling Method

The study employed purposive sampling to select participants who had direct involvement and knowledge of parish record management. The participants included:

- Parish Priest/Administrator (1): The primary decision-maker responsible for approving record requests and overseeing parish operations.
- Parish Staff/Secretary (2-3): Personnel responsible for day-to-day record management, data entry, and certificate issuance.
- Parishioners (5-8): Regular churchgoers who have previously requested sacramental certificates, providing the end-user perspective on service quality and waiting times.

The criteria for selection included: (1) at least one year of experience working with parish records for staff members; (2) familiarity with the current manual system; and (3) willingness to participate in interviews and system testing. This sampling method ensured that the data gathered was relevant and comprehensive, capturing insights from all stakeholder groups who would interact with the proposed system.

3.2.3 Statistical Treatment of Data

The data collected from interviews and observations were analyzed using qualitative methods, specifically thematic analysis. Responses were transcribed, coded, and categorized into themes such as "challenges in record retrieval," "common errors," "security concerns," and "desired system features." Frequency counts were used to identify the most commonly mentioned issues and requirements.

For the system evaluation phase, quantitative data was collected using a Likert scale questionnaire (1-5, where 1 = Strongly Disagree and 5 = Strongly Agree) to measure user satisfaction with system features including OCR accuracy, search functionality, ease of use, and overall system performance. Descriptive statistics including mean scores and percentages were computed to summarize user feedback. The interpretation scale used was: 4.21-5.00 (Strongly Agree/Excellent), 3.41-4.20 (Agree/Good), 2.61-3.40 (Neutral/Fair), 1.81-2.60 (Disagree/Poor), and 1.00-1.80 (Strongly Disagree/Very Poor).


3.3 Description of the Current System (Information Gathering)

The existing system at the parish is a manual, paper-based record management process that has been in place for several decades. Under this system, all sacramental records including baptism, marriage, confirmation, and death records are handwritten or typewritten on physical record books and paper forms.

The current workflow begins when a parishioner requests a sacramental certificate. The staff member must manually search through physical record books organized by year and sacrament type. For baptismal records, the search may involve reviewing multiple large registry books if the exact date is unknown. Once the record is located, the staff manually transcribes the information onto a certificate form, which requires careful attention to avoid transcription errors. The certificate is then signed by the parish priest and sealed before release to the requestor.

The tools currently used include physical record books (some dating back several decades), filing cabinets for supporting documents, carbon paper for duplicate copies, and typewriters or handwriting for preparing certificates. Storage conditions vary, with some older records showing signs of deterioration due to humidity, paper aging, and occasional pest damage.

The limitations of the current system are significant. Searching for records can take anywhere from 15 minutes to several hours depending on the volume of records and whether the requestor remembers the approximate year of the sacrament. There is a high risk of human error during manual transcription, which can result in incorrect names, dates, or other critical information appearing on official certificates. Paper records are vulnerable to damage from fire, flood, or simple deterioration over time, with no backup copies available for most historical records. Additionally, the system cannot easily generate reports or analytics about sacrament trends, making it difficult for parish leadership to plan programs or allocate resources effectively. The physical nature of the records also limits access to authorized personnel who must be physically present in the parish office during working hours.


3.4 System Architecture and Development

This section presents the technical foundation and structural design of the Parish Operational Records Management System with ML Kit OCR. It describes how the system is organized into functional components, the technologies employed in its development, and the operational workflow that enables efficient management of sacramental records. The discussion covers the system's high-level architecture, specific objectives it aims to achieve, the hardware and software requirements necessary for implementation, and the detailed flow of activities from user access to data persistence. Understanding the system architecture is essential for comprehending how the various components interact to deliver a cohesive, secure, and scalable solution that addresses the limitations of the current manual record-keeping process.

3.4.1 Overview of the Proposed System

The Parish Operational Records Management System with ML Kit OCR is a comprehensive digital solution designed to modernize and streamline the management of sacramental records. The system is built as a cross-platform application using Flutter, supporting both mobile app and web based interfaces, ensuring accessibility for different user preferences and device capabilities.

The system features a three-tier architecture consisting of the presentation layer (Flutter frontend), business logic layer (Node.js backend API and Firebase Cloud Functions), and data layer (Firebase Firestore and Cloud Storage). This architecture ensures separation of concerns, scalability, and maintainability.

Key features include: (1) ML Kit OCR integration for scanning and extracting text from paper-based sacramental documents, automatically populating digital record fields; (2) a centralized digital repository for all sacramental records with robust search and filter capabilities; (3) role-based access control supporting Admin, Staff, and User roles with appropriate permissions; (4) a request and notification system for certificate requests; (5) household management linking family members to their sacramental records; and (6) audit logging for tracking all system activities.

The primary users of the system are: Parish Administrators who manage user accounts, approve requests, oversee system operations, and access financial reports; Parish Staff who handle day-to-day record entry, OCR processing, and certificate preparation; Finance Staff who manage donations, track certificate fees, and generate financial reports; Parishioners who can request certificates, view their household information, and access public landing page features; and Visitors who can view public information on the landing page without authentication.

3.4.2 System Objectives

The system objectives align with the general and specific objectives outlined in Chapter 1, focusing on delivering a functional, efficient, and user-friendly record management solution:

1. To provide a structured and efficient search mechanism that allows users to locate specific parish records within seconds rather than minutes or hours, using multiple search criteria including name, date range, sacrament type, and household membership.

2. To successfully integrate Google ML Kit OCR technology for extracting text from scanned sacramental documents with high accuracy, reducing manual data entry workload by automatically recognizing and populating record fields such as names, dates, and sacrament details.

3. To develop an intuitive web-based platform accessible across devices (mobile and desktop) that enables parish staff to efficiently manage record submissions, updates, and certificate generation while providing parishioners with a self-service portal for requesting documents.

4. To implement a comprehensive role-based access control system that enforces appropriate permissions for different user types (Admin, Staff, Finance, User), ensuring data security and preventing unauthorized access to sensitive sacramental information.

5. To significantly reduce manual workload and minimize human errors in data entry by providing automated OCR processing, validation rules, and standardized record formats, thereby improving overall operational efficiency by at least 60% compared to the manual system.

3.4.4 System Flow/Functions

The System Architecture Flow Chart (Figure 3a) illustrates the complete operational flow of the Parish Records Management System from initial access through task completion. The following describes the input-process-output sequence and user interactions at each stage:

**Phase 1: System Entry and Authentication**

*Input:*
- User accesses the system via mobile application (Android) or web interface
- User indicates whether they have an existing account

*Process:*
- System displays landing interface
- Decision point: "Have Account?"
  - If No: Redirect to Sign Up page for account creation
  - If Yes: Redirect to Login page
- User enters credentials (email and password)
- Firebase Authentication validates credentials against stored user data
- Decision point: "Valid Credentials?"
  - If No: Display error message, return to login
  - If Yes: Proceed to role verification

*Output:*
- Authenticated user session with valid Firebase ID token
- User role identified (Admin, Staff, Parishioner, Finance, or Visitor)
- Redirect to role-appropriate dashboard

**Phase 2: Role-Based Access and Operations**

The system branches into five distinct user pathways based on role assignment:

*Path A: Administrator Operations*

*Input:*
- Admin dashboard access request
- Administrative commands (create user, assign role, disable account)
- Analytics queries and report requests

*Process:*
- Verify admin privileges through role middleware
- User Management operations:
  - Create new user accounts with invitation codes
  - Edit existing user information and roles
  - Disable/enable user accounts
- System Reporting:
  - Generate analytics on record statistics
  - View audit logs for all system activities
  - Export system data for external analysis
- All actions logged to Audit Logs (D9) with timestamp and user ID

*Output:*
- Updated user accounts in Users collection (D1)
- Audit trail entries in Audit Logs (D9)
- System reports and analytics displayed on dashboard
- Notifications sent to affected users

*Path B: Staff Operations*

*Input:*
- Document image from camera or file upload
- Manual record entry data
- Household registration information
- Certificate request processing commands

*Process:*
- OCR Processing (3.0):
  - Image uploaded to Firebase Storage
  - Google ML Kit extracts text from document
  - Extracted data mapped to record fields (name, date, sacrament type)
  - Staff verifies and corrects extracted information
  - Data stored in Scanned Documents (D6)
- Record Management (2.0):
  - Create new sacramental records (Baptism, Marriage, Confirmation, Death)
  - Edit existing records with validation
  - Search records by name, date, or sacrament type
  - Link records to household members
- Household Management:
  - Register new households with family details
  - Add/edit household members
  - Associate sacramental records with members
- Request Processing:
  - Review certificate requests from parishioners
  - Approve or reject requests with status updates
  - Generate digital certificates for approved requests

*Output:*
- New records in Sacramental Records (D3)
- Updated household data in Households (D4) and Household Members (D5)
- Processed certificate requests in Requests (D7)
- Notifications sent to requesting parishioners

*Path C: Parishioner (User) Operations*

*Input:*
- Profile update information
- Household viewing requests
- Certificate request submissions
- Document upload for verification

*Process:*
- User Registration/Profile Management:
  - Complete personal profile information
  - View linked household and family members
  - View personal sacramental records
- Certificate Request Workflow:
  - Submit request with purpose (baptismal copy, marriage requirement, etc.)
  - Track request status (Pending, Processing, Approved, Rejected)
  - Download generated certificate upon approval
- Notification Reception:
  - Receive push notifications on request status changes
  - View in-app alerts for system announcements

*Output:*
- Updated user profile in Users (D1)
- New certificate request in Requests (D7)
- Downloadable certificate document
- Real-time status updates via notifications

*Path D: Finance Operations*

*Input:*
- Donation recording data (amount, donor, purpose, date)
- Certificate fee payment information
- Financial report queries

*Process:*
- Donation Management:
  - Record cash and online donations
  - Categorize by fund (building, charity, operational)
  - Generate donation receipts
- Fee Tracking:
  - Record certificate fee payments
  - Track outstanding balances
  - Match fees to processed requests
- Financial Reporting:
  - Generate donation summaries by period
  - Create fee collection reports
  - Export data for accounting integration

*Output:*
- Donation records in Donations (D13)
- Fee records in Fees (D14)
- Financial reports for parish administration
- Receipts generated for donors

*Path E: Visitor Access (No Authentication)*

*Input:*
- Public access request to landing page

*Process:*
- Display Landing Page content:
  - Parish announcements and news
  - Sacrament schedules (Mass times, confession schedules)
  - Parish contact information and location
  - Online services overview
- Account Request:
  - Visitors may submit account creation requests
  - Request routed to Administrator for approval

*Output:*
- Public information displayed without login
- Account request sent to Admin panel

**Phase 3: Data Persistence and Backend Processing**

*Input:*
- All data modification operations from user processes
- File uploads (images, documents)
- Authentication token validation requests

*Process:*
- Firestore Database Operations:
  - Write validated data to appropriate collections (D1-D14)
  - Maintain referential integrity between records and households
  - Update indexes for search functionality
  - Enforce security rules at document level
- Backend Services:
  - Node.js API handles business logic
  - Firebase Functions trigger on data changes
  - Cloud Storage manages document images
  - ML Kit OCR processes text extraction
- Audit Logging:
  - Every create, update, delete operation logged
  - User ID, timestamp, action type, and changes recorded
  - Audit trail available for compliance review

*Output:*
- Persisted data across all collections
- Synchronized data accessible in real-time
- Backup copies in Firebase Cloud
- Complete audit trail for accountability

**Phase 4: Session Termination**

*Input:*
- User selects logout option
- Session timeout occurs

*Process:*
- Firebase Authentication signs out user
- Local session data cleared
- Decision point: "Logout?"
  - If Yes: Terminate session, return to Start
  - If No: Continue current session

*Output:*
- Session ended securely
- User redirected to login page or landing page
- All unsaved data prompts for confirmation

The flow chart demonstrates a structured workflow where:
1. All users must authenticate (except Visitors on landing page)
2. Role-based routing ensures appropriate access control
3. Each role performs specialized operations tied to specific data stores
4. All data modifications persist to Firestore Database
5. Backend services support OCR, authentication, and notifications
6. Audit logging maintains accountability for all actions
7. Users exit through secure logout process

This architecture ensures data security through Firebase Authentication, maintains data integrity through Firestore transactions, and provides real-time synchronization across all user interfaces.

3.4.5 Scope and Limitations of the System

Scope:

The Parish Operational Records Management System with ML Kit OCR includes the following capabilities:

- Digital storage and management of four primary sacramental record types: Baptism, Marriage, Confirmation, and Death (Funeral) records
- ML Kit OCR integration for automated text extraction from scanned paper documents, supporting both printed and handwritten text recognition
- Complete record lifecycle management including creation, viewing, searching, updating, and soft-deletion of records
- Comprehensive household management system with family member profiles and sacramental record linkage
- Role-based access control with four primary roles: Administrator (full system access), Staff (record management and processing), Finance (donation and fee tracking), and User (certificate requests and household viewing)
- Certificate request workflow with status tracking and notification system
- Audit logging for all record modifications, access events, and administrative actions
- Analytics dashboard for administrators showing record statistics and system usage metrics
- Cross-platform accessibility through Flutter Android mobile application and responsive web-based interface
- Firebase Cloud integration for real-time data synchronization, secure authentication, and scalable storage
- **Public Landing Page** providing parish information, announcements, sacrament schedules, and contact details accessible without authentication
- **Financial Management** module for recording donations, tracking certificate fees, and generating basic financial reports

Limitations:

The system has the following limitations:

- OCR accuracy depends on document quality; poor image resolution, faded text, or heavily stylized fonts may result in extraction errors requiring manual correction
- The system is designed as an online platform and requires active internet connectivity for all functions
- Legal compliance with all jurisdiction-specific requirements is not automatically guaranteed and requires proper configuration and legal review
- Data accuracy is dependent on the quality of source documents and correctness of manual entries or OCR corrections
- System performance and scalability are constrained by Firebase quotas, query patterns, and backend resources; extremely large parishes with millions of records may require additional optimization
- Advanced offline synchronization is not implemented as the system operates entirely online; all data access and modifications require internet connectivity
- The finance module provides basic donation and fee tracking but does not replace full-featured accounting systems or support complex financial operations
- The landing page displays public information only; authenticated features require login
- Notification delivery depends on external services (Firebase Cloud Messaging) and user device settings; delivery is not guaranteed in all network conditions
- The system does not include automatic backup to non-Firebase storage; organizations requiring redundant backup strategies must implement additional measures
- ML Kit OCR performance varies by device capability; older mobile devices may experience slower processing times

Database Design Principles:
- Denormalization: Selected data duplication (e.g., fullName in household_members) to optimize read performance for common queries
- Top-Level Collections: All entities stored as top-level collections (users, records, households, household_members, etc.) for better query flexibility and security rules
- Indexing: Composite indexes configured for common query patterns (date ranges, type filters, status filters)
- Soft Deletes: isDeleted/isArchived flags used instead of hard deletion to maintain data integrity and audit trail
- Timestamps: All collections include createdAt/updatedAt for temporal tracking
- References: String IDs used for cross-collection references (e.g., householdId in household_members) rather than Firestore document references for flexibility

Use Case Descriptions:

Administrator Use Cases:
- UC-001: Manage Users - Create, edit, delete user accounts; assign roles (Admin, Staff, Finance, User); enable/disable accounts
- UC-002: View Analytics - Access dashboard showing record statistics, user activity, request trends, and system usage metrics
- UC-003: Process Requests - Review certificate requests submitted by parishioners; approve or reject with notes
- UC-004: Send Notifications - Broadcast announcements to all users or specific groups; send targeted notifications
- UC-005: View Audit Logs - Review system activity logs for accountability, compliance, and security monitoring
- UC-006: Manage System Settings - Configure parish information, notification preferences, and system parameters
- UC-007: View All Records - Access any sacramental record in the system regardless of household association

Staff Use Cases:
- UC-008: Create Records - Manually enter new sacramental records (baptism, marriage, confirmation, death)
- UC-009: Search Records - Find records using filters (name, date, sacrament type, household)
- UC-010: Edit Records - Update existing record information; correct errors; add remarks
- UC-011: Manage Households - Register new households; add/edit family members; link members to records
- UC-012: Process Requests - Review and fulfill certificate requests; update request status; prepare certificates
- UC-013: OCR Scan Documents - Capture document images; process with ML Kit OCR; verify extracted text
- UC-014: Generate Certificates - Create official certificate documents with proper formatting and signatures

User (Parishioner) Use Cases:
- UC-015: Register Account - Create account using invitation code; complete profile information
- UC-016: View Household - View own family information and member list; view linked sacramental records
- UC-017: Request Certificate - Submit requests for baptismal, marriage, confirmation, or death certificates
- UC-018: View Request Status - Track progress of submitted requests; view history of past requests
- UC-019: Update Profile - Manage personal information, contact details, and notification preferences
- UC-020: Receive Notifications - Get updates about request status, system announcements, and parish news

Finance Staff Use Cases:
- UC-021: Record Donations - Enter donation details including donor information, amount, type, and purpose; generate donation receipts
- UC-022: View Donation Reports - Access donation summaries by date range, donor, or purpose; export donation data
- UC-023: Track Certificate Fees - Record fee payments associated with certificate requests; manage payment status
- UC-024: Generate Financial Reports - Create basic financial summaries combining donations and fee collections

Visitor (Unauthenticated) Use Cases:
- UC-025: View Landing Page - Access public parish information without authentication
- UC-026: View Announcements - Read public announcements and parish news
- UC-027: View Sacrament Schedules - Access mass times and sacrament schedules
- UC-028: View Parish Information - Access contact details, office hours, and parish history
- UC-029: Request Account - Submit request for parishioner account registration

- Edit Records (update existing record information)

- Process Requests (review and fulfill certificate requests)

User (Parishioner) Actor:

Visitor Actor:

Symbol Usage in Diagram:

Flow Description:
=================
1. START: User accesses the system (Mobile App/Web)
2. USER ACCESS: User attempts to login or register
3. AUTHENTICATE: Firebase Authentication verifies credentials
4. CHECK ROLE: System determines user role (Admin, Staff, User, Finance, Visitor)
5. DASHBOARD ACCESS: User redirected to role-specific dashboard

Admin Flow: User Management → Reports/Analytics → Database
Staff Flow: OCR Scanning → Record Management → Household Management → Database
User Flow: Registration → Certificate Request → Notifications → Database
Finance Flow: Donation Recording → Fee Tracking → Financial Reports → Database
Visitor Flow: Direct access to Landing Page (no auth required)

All authenticated flows connect to Firestore Database and Backend Services
for data persistence and business logic processing.

DATA FLOW DESCRIPTIONS:
=======================

Flow 1: Authentication Flow
  Source: Users (Admin, Staff, Parishioner)
  Process: 1.0 Authentication Management
  Data Stores: D1 (Users), D2 (Invites)
  Description: Login credentials → Validation → Auth tokens/Session creation

Flow 2: Record Management Flow
  Source: Staff User
  Process: 2.0 Record Management
  Data Stores: D3 (Sacramental_Records), D4 (Households), D5 (Household_Members)
  Description: Record CRUD operations, household registration, member linking

Flow 3: OCR Processing Flow
  Source: Staff User → External: Google ML Kit OCR
  Process: 3.0 OCR Processing
  Data Store: D6 (Scanned_Documents)
  Description: Image upload → ML Kit processing → Extracted text → Record population

Flow 4: Request Workflow Flow
  Source: Parishioner → Admin/Staff
  Process: 4.0 Request Management
  Data Store: D7 (Requests)
  Description: Certificate request submission → Review → Approval/Rejection → Status update

Flow 5: Notification Flow
  Source: System Processes / Administrator
  Process: 5.0 Notification System
  Data Store: D8 (Notifications)
  Destination: Users (via FCM, EmailJS)
  Description: Triggers → Notification creation → Multi-channel delivery

Flow 6: Administration Flow
  Source: Administrator
  Process: 6.0 Administration & Reporting
  Data Store: D9 (Audit_Logs)
  Description: Admin actions → Audit logging → Reports/Analytics generation

Flow 7: Landing Page Flow
  Source: Visitor
  Process: 7.0 Landing Page Management
  Data Stores: D10 (Announcements), D11 (Parish_Info), D12 (Sacrament_Schedules)
  Description: Public access request → Content retrieval → Display of announcements, parish info, schedules

Flow 8: Financial Management Flow
  Source: Finance Staff
  Process: 8.0 Financial Management
  Data Stores: D13 (Donations), D14 (Fees)
  Description: Donation/Fee data entry → Financial record storage → Report generation and analytics

Data Flow Summary:
==================
The comprehensive DFD illustrates how data moves between external entities (users), 
processes (numbered 1.0-8.0), and data stores (D1-D14) within the Firestore Database.

Key Data Flows:
• Authentication (1.0): Validates users against D1 (Users) and D2 (Invites)
• Records (2.0): Manages sacramental data in D3-D5
• OCR (3.0): Processes images via Google ML Kit into D6
• Requests (4.0): Handles certificate workflows in D7
• Notifications (5.0): Delivers messages via FCM/Email
• Admin (6.0): Generates reports from D9 (Audit Logs)
• Landing Page (7.0): Serves public content from D10-D12
• Finance (8.0): Records transactions in D13-D14

External Services:
• Firebase Auth: User authentication and token generation
• Google ML Kit: OCR text extraction from scanned documents
• FCM/EmailJS: Push notifications and email delivery
